03

Wrap-around systems

Not a layer — these run through the whole vehicle. Skip one and the build is unsafe, illegal, or unsellable.

▣ Safety cage

Isolation

→ Security & multi-tenancy

RBAC + row-level isolation stop one tenant crashing into another's data. The cargo is PII — this keeps you out of jail.

◉ Black box

Audit

→ Immutable event log

Tamper-evident record of everything. Required after an accident. Bolt it into the chassis day one — no retrofit after the crash.

◐ Region-spec

Locale

→ Localization & jurisdiction

Same model, re-homologated per country: leave accrual, statutory exits, tax. Where most hidden cost lives.

⛭ Assembly line

Templates

→ Tenant provisioning

Every tenant rolls off one line from a build sheet, sharing standard parts. Upgrades = an over-the-air recall.

05

Two decisions that sink builds

The gearbox you machine yourself, and the accessories you weld in "because they might be useful." Both are mistakes.

⚙ Gearbox · buy, don't build

Payroll

→ Payroll & compensation

The most precision-critical, most regulated subsystem in the whole vehicle. Note that most automakers don't machine their own transmissions — they source from ZF or Aisin. Right now payroll is missing from the design entirely.

Own the comp data. Integrate payroll execution. Don't build the gearbox.
⊕ Roof box · accessory, not core

Satellites

→ LMS · Travel · Asset tracking

Each is a whole product — a quarter of work for maybe 10% of customers. They're the roof box and tow hitch: bolt-on, optional, most drivers never fit them. Forcing them into core sinks the roadmap.

Mount on accessory ports (plugins) — or integrate a specialist. Never weld to the frame.
